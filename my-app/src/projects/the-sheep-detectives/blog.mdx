---
title: "Surviving Impostor Syndrome and Quadruped Weight"
subtitle: "How joining Panot to animate remotely on Framestore's systems became a masterclass in quadruped weight and realistic timing."
---

Joining Panot was absolute pure luck. On a Friday, I finished up my previous project in Berlin, packed my entire life into a couple of bags, and by Monday morning, I was sitting in the Barcelona studio, not knowing a single soul. It was a complete whirlwind. When I walked in, I met the team and my supervisor, [Ferran Casas](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ferran-casas-23b2292), and immediately felt a massive wave of panic. Every single animator in that room had way more experience than me. They had all worked on huge, big-budget feature films before, and most of them already knew each other and had this amazing, established shorthand. Meanwhile, I was just trying to figure out where the coffee machine was and how to connect remotely to Framestore's massive, mind-bogglingly confusing pipeline.

For the first month and a half, I was navigating a pretty solid dose of impostor syndrome, just trying to get my bearings among all these veterans. The ultimate test of patience was my very first shot in the movie... animating a character named **WinterLamb**. It was quite the struggle just to get that first shot approved.

Yes, that was the line. And honestly, the timing couldn't have been more perfect. While WinterLamb was crying out on my monitor about being loved, I was sitting there in my own little bubble of impostor syndrome, sweating through my shirt and half-convinced my next set of keyframes would get me sent back to Berlin. The contrast was hilarious in hindsight; this tiny, dramatic lamb crying for affection while I was staring at a massive, complex skeleton rig just hoping my animation curves made sense.

But I got incredibly lucky. Meeting Ferran Casas was a lifesaver. He is easily one of the funniest, most practical, and genuinely kind supervisors I have ever met. No matter how much I struggled or how nervous I got, Ferran never gave up on me. He would pull up a chair, crack a joke to break my tension, and patiently show me how realistic quadruped weight shifts actually work.

Slowly, through those sessions and the daily reviews with [Chris Hurtt](https://www.linkedin.com/in/hurtt/), I started to find my footing. Reviews from Chris became masterclasses in micro-details, teaching me how to transition from my stylized cartoon habits into ultra-realistic animal motion. I began to understand the confusing pipeline, the tools clicked, and my confidence grew alongside this wonderful crew of veterans.
